CubeSat for Real-Time Weather Prediction

The CubeSat for Real-Time Weather Prediction project is an innovative step toward improving the way we monitor and forecast weather conditions. Using a low-cost, lightweight satellite model (CubeSat) mounted on a drone, we aim to create a scalable, accessible platform for atmospheric data collection. Unlike traditional weather monitoring systems, this CubeSat integrates advanced sensors, LoRa communication, and AI-based prediction models to gather real-time data such as temperature, humidity and pressure. The data is then processed through machine learning algorithms to enhance accuracy and provide reliable short-term forecasts. Our system is designed to be modular and open-source, encouraging improvements and adaptations by future innovators. With a Raspberry Pi core and robust communication links, the CubeSat enables students and researchers to experiment with weather analysis, sensor fusion, and AI in aerospace applications. This project not only demonstrates the potential of CubeSats for Earth observation but also highlights how small, cost-effective platforms can contribute to solving real-world challenges like climate monitoring, disaster prediction, and environmental studies. By combining aerospace engineering, electronics, AI/ML, and open-source collaboration, the CubeSat for Real-Time Weather Prediction sets the foundation for the next generation of research and innovation in atmospheric science. üåçüì°

Design and Development of an RC Fixed-Wing Aircraft

The Design and Development of an RC Fixed-Wing Aircraft project was our entry point into the world of aeronautics and model aircraft engineering. With curiosity as our guide and a determination to learn, we set out to design and fly our very own RC plane‚Äîusing online resources, YouTube tutorials, and continuous trial-and-error experiments. The process began with understanding the basics of flight‚Äîaerodynamic principles, lift generation, thrust-to-weight ratios, and stability control. With these foundations, we started building our fixed-wing aircraft using lightweight materials and readily available components. Every stage of the project‚Äîcutting, assembling, wiring, and balancing‚Äîtaught us something new. While not every attempt resulted in a perfect flight, each trial gave us valuable insights into airframe design, control surfaces, and propulsion systems. Slowly, through adjustments and refinements, we transformed our initial prototypes into a fully functional RC fixed-wing plane capable of controlled flight. This project helped us develop practical problem-solving skills, teamwork, and resilience, while strengthening our understanding of aeronautics far beyond classroom theory. It also marked the first step in our journey toward advanced UAVs and aerospace projects, building confidence that we can design, test, and improve through persistence and innovation. An AI/ML Framework for Smart Data Prediction

The AI/ML Framework for Smart Data Prediction project aims to revolutionize how we analyze and interpret complex datasets within aerospace and other domains. We're developing a robust, open-source framework that leverages artificial intelligence and machine learning to predict trends, identify anomalies, and make informed decisions from large volumes of data. Our framework is designed to be versatile, supporting various applications such as: Predictive maintenance for aircraft and drone components, minimizing downtime and increasing safety. Real-time flight path optimization, factoring in weather patterns and air traffic data. Enhanced data analysis for CubeSats, allowing faster processing of Earth observation data. Automated anomaly detection in sensor readings from aerial vehicles, providing early warnings for potential issues. This project focuses on creating a user-friendly and modular system where developers and researchers can easily integrate new AI/ML models and adapt the framework to specific data challenges. By combining cutting-edge algorithms with an intuitive interface, we're building a tool that simplifies data science and makes advanced prediction accessible to a wider audience. The AI/ML Framework is more than just a software project; it's a step toward smart, data-driven aerospace engineering. It empowers our members to explore the convergence of AI and aeronautics, pushing the boundaries of what's possible in intelligent systems and data analytics. üöÄüß†

ARDUINO BASED OBJECT AVOIDANCE CAR

The Arduino-Based Object Avoidance Car project was one of our first steps into the world of electronics, robotics, and embedded systems. Our goal was not just to build a moving car, but to understand how sensors, microcontrollers, and actuators work together in a real-world application. Using an Arduino board as the brain, ultrasonic sensors to detect obstacles, and simple motor driver circuits for movement, we built a car that could navigate its surroundings and automatically avoid collisions. The project introduced us to the fundamentals of coding, wiring, circuit design, and sensor integration, helping us see how each part plays a role in the bigger system. More than just a working prototype, this project was an essential learning experience. It helped us build confidence in handling basic electronics, Arduino programming, and debugging, while also teaching us how important it is to test, adjust, and improve step by step. The Object Avoidance Car may have been small in scale, but it laid the foundation for our future work in robotics, drones, and autonomous systems‚Äîshowing us how simple components can come together to create intelligent behavior. üöóü§ñ
